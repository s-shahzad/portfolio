ML-Based DoS Detection for EV Charging Infrastructure

Context: IEEE GCAIoT 2025 publication | Domain: IoT security and connected charging systems

Designed an ML-driven intrusion-detection approach focused on denial-of-service risk in EV charging environments, then formalized the work in an IEEE publication.

Signal

Problem

Problem

EV charging infrastructure introduces a broader attack surface, and denial-of-service activity can disrupt availability across connected systems that depend on continuous service.

Approach

  • Framed the EV charging environment as an intrusion-detection problem with an emphasis on traffic-based denial-of-service indicators.
  • Applied a machine-learning-oriented detection approach to classify suspicious behavior patterns.
  • Structured the work into a publishable, defensible research artifact that could communicate the security model clearly.

Outcome

The project strengthened detection readiness for connected charging systems and produced a concrete research-backed framing for how to approach DoS risk in this environment.

Business Value

Beyond the publication itself, the value is in showing the ability to combine applied security thinking, ML-assisted detection design, and technical communication that can stand up to external review.
